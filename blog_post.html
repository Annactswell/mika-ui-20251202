10 Tips for Better Prompts: Stop Getting Six Fingers

Felix

We've all been there. You type "beautiful hand holding an apple," and the AI gives you a mutant claw with 7 fingers. Fear not. Here is the definitive guide to prompting like a pro.

1. Be Specific with Styles

Don't just say "art". Say "oil painting by Van Gogh" or "cyberpunk digital art, trending on ArtStation". The model needs a visual reference point to anchor the generation.

"Prompting is not about describing the subject. It's about describing the *image* of the subject."

2. Negative Prompts are Your Best Friend

Use the --no parameter or the negative prompt box.

  • Bad anatomy: Fixes limbs and faces.
  • Blurry: Keeps things crisp.
  • Watermark: Removes accidental text.
  • Mutated hands: Crucial for portraits.

3. Weighting Matters

If you want more emphasis on a word, use brackets. (cyberpunk:1.2) tells the engine to focus 20% more on that concept. Use this sparinglyâ€”too much weight can burn the image.

Example of weighting: (Neon:1.5)

4. Lighting is Everything

Terms like "volumetric lighting," "cinematic lighting," "golden hour," and "studio lighting" dramatically change the mood. Treat the AI like a photographer.
